## What worked, what didn't, why

### Wins

- **9-TCL prompt-template averaging (+1.63 mIoU alone, +1.11 on top of values)** — averaging text embeddings across the 9 templates from the official TIPSv2 notebook (`itap of a {}.`, `a bad photo of a {}.`, `a origami {}.`, `a photo of the large {}.`, `a {} in a video game.`, `art of the {}.`, `a photo of the small {}.`, `a photo of many {}.`, `a photo of {}s.`). Cheap, strict win. L2-normalise each embedding before averaging.
- **Values trick (+4.96 mIoU alone)** — replace last attention block with `ls1(proj(v))` and skip the MLP residual entirely. Standard MaskCLIP / TIPSv2 official `encode_image_value_attention`. Single biggest win in the matrix.
- **M-SA modulated self-attention (+0.50 over plain values)** — SegEarth-OV (CVPR'25) Eq. 10. Replace last attention's softmax(qkᵀ)·v with the SUM of three self-self attentions: `softmax(qq) + softmax(kk) + softmax(vv)`, applied to v. Combines the q-q insight from SCLIP with the self-self pattern from MaskCLIP. Small but consistent +0.5–0.9 mIoU gain.

### Surprising regressions

- **CLS-bias subtraction (λ=0.3) HURT TIPSv2** (−2.03 with values, ~neutral with M-SA). SegEarth-OV's Eq. 9 was tuned for OpenAI CLIP-B/16. Hypothesis: TIPSv2's *register tokens* (1 register slot in the model config) already absorb the global content that the CLS token contaminates patch tokens with in plain CLIP. Subtracting again over-corrects. Worth re-checking with smaller λ (e.g., 0.1) but not pursued in this session.
- **ClearCLIP residual removal: small mIoU loss, big pixel-acc gain.** −0.35 mIoU, +2.6 pp pixel acc. Trade-off, not a free win. Suggests it sharpens dominant classes at the cost of small-class boundaries — useful if the product cares about whole-scene labels, less so for fine boundaries.

### Why values + M-SA ≠ raw additive

Values trick alone: +4.96 mIoU. M-SA alone over baseline: not directly measured but extrapolating it's ~+5.5 (M-SA gave +0.5 over values, and values gave +4.96, so M-SA over baseline ≈ +5.46). So M-SA does not stack additively with the values trick — they're competing parameterisations of the same last-block modification. M-SA wins on its own.

## Code surface

```python
_encode_image_dense(model, pixel_values, *,
                    attn_mode={"default","values","msa"},
                    cls_subtract=0.0,         # λ for SegEarth-OV Eq. 9
```

## RS-VLM landscape (what I read while benches ran)

Surveyed in this session via the papers Ziming linked + WebSearch:

| Family | Examples | Task family | Status for Roger Studio |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| RS-specific VLMs (conversational) | GeoChat, EarthDial, RS-LLaVA, SkySenseGPT, LHRS-Bot-Nova, **GeoVLM-R1** (Sep'25) | Q&A, captioning, classification, detection | **Different product slot** — LLM tab, not the segmentation tab |
| RS-specific VLMs (segmentation) | SegEarth-OV (CVPR'25, CLIP-B/16) → **39.2 % mIoU avg** on 8 RS seg datasets | Training-free dense seg | Closest to our path. Its tricks (M-SA, CLS subtract) transferred partially to TIPSv2; M-SA was the win. |
| RS-specific VLMs (segmentation, latest) | **SegEarth-OV3** (Dec'25, SAM 3 backbone) → **53.4 % mIoU avg** | Training-free dense seg + change detection + 3D | Different foundation model entirely. Worth considering as a parallel path; not a drop-in modification. |
| Generic VLMs benched on RS | GLaMM (per GEOBench-VLM) | Referring segmentation | 14.11 % mIoU. We're not directly comparable on this task family. |
| Generic VLMs benched on RS (Q&A) | LLaVA-OneVision, GPT-4o, Qwen2-VL, InternVL-2 | MCQ accuracy | LLaVA-OneVision leads at 41.7 % (random ~25 %). |

Key takeaway from the landscape: **dense semantic segmentation on RS imagery is still an open problem**. The RS-specific models in production today (GeoChat, EarthDial, GeoVLM-R1) don't do per-pixel masks — they do conversational outputs. The pipelines that do produce pixel masks (SegEarth-OV family) are training-free CLIP/TIPSv2-style approaches like ours. So Roger Studio's TIPSv2 segmentation tab is in the right family; the gap is to the SegEarth-OV3 (SAM 3) frontier, not to a different VLM paradigm.

## Strategic recommendations for Roger Studio

1. **Ship the M-SA setup as the new default for the TIPSv2 tab.** 25.87 mIoU on ADE20K is the frontier of CLIP-style zero-shot dense seg today; matches/beats published TIPSv2-L/14 reference. Surface `attn_mode="msa"` as the default in `auto_label_geotiff_tipsv2`.
2. **Don't chase ADE20K further — bench on RS instead.** The ADE20K number is a self-consistency check, not a product metric. Roger Studio users feed satellite tiles, not web/indoor imagery. Wire up a LoveDA-or-equivalent bench (5,987 RS images, 7 classes) and see what the actual product metric is.
3. **Keep CLS-subtract / drop-residual as off-by-default knobs.** They didn't help on ADE20K but the SegEarth-OV ablations show they help on RS (with CLIP-B/16). Worth re-benching once the LoveDA path exists.
4. **Watch SegEarth-OV3.** SAM 3 + their pipeline reports 53.4 % avg mIoU on 8 RS datasets — a +14 point jump over SegEarth-OV. If RS accuracy is the bottleneck for Roger Studio, adding a SAM-3-backed pipeline alongside TIPSv2 (rather than replacing TIPSv2) is the most promising direction. Two backends, user picks.
5. **Don't chase reasoning-VLMs (GeoVLM-R1, EarthDial) for the segmentation tab.** They're for the LLM/Chat tab. Different product surface.

The 2-scale variant slightly regressed (averaging predictions from a downsized image hurt more than it helped), but adding an UPsampled scale (1.25×) lifted us by +0.63 at n=200. **Confirmed on full 2000: +0.46 mIoU (25.87 → 26.33)** and +0.75 pixel acc (52.96 → 53.71). **3-scale costs ~3× wall time** for the gain — worth it for "best number" benching, possibly not for production inference where users want sub-second polygons.

## Counter-intuitive: g/14 (~1B params) is WORSE than L/14 (~303M)

| Model | n | mIoU | pixel acc | Config |
| --- | ---:| ---:| ---:| --- |
| L/14 + 9-tpl + M-SA | 2000 | **25.87 %** | 52.96 % | best |
| g/14 + 9-tpl + M-SA | 2000 | 20.13 % | 37.31 % | −5.74 vs L/14 |
| g/14 + 9-tpl + values | 200 | 17.18 % | 31.84 % | worse than M-SA |
| g/14 + 9-tpl + default (no last-block mod) | 200 | 15.08 % | 28.33 % | worst |

**Why this matters for Roger Studio:** the natural assumption is "bigger model = better". For dense seg with our pipeline, that's wrong. **Stick with L/14.** Reasons we suspect:

1. **g/14 uses SwiGLU FFN** (`ffn_layer: "swiglu"` in config.json) where B/14 and L/14 use plain MLP. The values/M-SA tricks skip the last block's MLP residual; SwiGLU is doing more of the per-token reasoning, so skipping it is a bigger loss.
2. **Bigger ViTs are known to over-rely on the [CLS] / cls-pool features** for image-text alignment objectives — patch tokens carry proportionally less local info. The "values trick" works by extracting patch-local features; if those patch tokens are weaker carriers of local content in g/14, the trick yields less.
3. **Numerical precision**: g/14 may need FP32 for the patch-token similarity stage; our impl uses model defaults.

We did NOT pursue a fix because L/14 already meets/beats published reference. The gap to closing g/14 is open work, not blocking.

## OlmoEarth FT bench investigation (2026-04-30)

The user asked: what's our accuracy on the actual `allenai/olmoearth_projects_*` datasets? Followed up overnight. Status: **bench script written, no real number yet** — HTTP-through-running-backend approach hit infrastructure issues. Path forward documented below.

**What we learned about the datasets:**
- `allenai/olmoearth_projects_mangrove` — **100,000** labelled point samples (the "38" on HF is the file count, not sample count). Class dist Mangrove 45,850 / Other 29,973 / Water 24,177. Distributed as `mangrove.tar` (~96 MB) containing rslearn windows under `mangrove_classification/windows/default/task_NNNN/layers/annotation/data.geojson` plus a flat `input.csv` with `(uid, lon, lat, ref_cls, task_name, start_time, end_time)`. Year-2020 data. **No imagery in the tar** — labels only; the FT pipeline has to fetch S2 from STAC for each location's bbox + time range.
- `allenai/olmoearth_projects_awf` — 76.9k samples, 10 classes (`woodland_forest`, `open_water`, `shrubland_savanna`, `herbaceous_wetland`, `grassland_barren`, `agriculture_settlement`, `montane_forest`, `lava_forest`, `urban_dense_development`, `nodata`). Spatial split 75/25 % via 128×128-pixel grid hash.
- Both FT models (`OlmoEarth-v1-FT-Mangrove-Base`, `OlmoEarth-v1-FT-AWF-Base`) are wired into the running backend (`/api/olmoearth/catalog` lists them), and Roger Studio's `olmoearth_ft.py` already knows their class names + colors.

**Bench script delivered: `backend/scripts/bench_olmoearth_mangrove.py`.** Architecture:
- Hash-based deterministic subsample of `input.csv` so re-runs hit the same rows.
- Per sample: POST to `/api/olmoearth/infer` with a small bbox around the point + the `2020-01-01/2020-12-31` date range FT-Mangrove was trained on.
- The endpoint runs the full FT pipeline (S2 STAC fetch, 12-month composite, FT forward).
- Decode the predicted color at the labelled pixel by fetching the response tile + nearest-neighbour matching against the response legend.
- Reduce to a `(ref → pred)` confusion matrix → per-class IoU + overall accuracy.

**What blocked an actual run during this session:**
1. Initial test calls returned `kind="stub"` (deterministic gradient, not real model). Two causes hit in sequence:
   - FT-Mangrove weights not yet downloaded → fixed by `huggingface_hub.snapshot_download(repo_id="allenai/OlmoEarth-v1-FT-Mangrove-Base")`.
   - `date_range` was being passed as a **list** `["2020-01-01","2020-12-31"]` but the service expects a **slash-separated string** `"2020-01-01/2020-12-31"` (it does `.split("/")` internally). Fixed in the bench script.
2. After the date_range fix, single inference calls timed out at 600 s through `requests.post` (backend log `'list' object has no attribute 'split'` errors stopped, but the new request didn't appear in the log either). Inference per sample is 1–4 minutes for full S2 fetch + 12-month composite + FT forward — expected, but the running uvicorn worker became unresponsive after several iterations of WatchFiles auto-reloading on script edits, which is the bigger issue.
3. **WatchFiles in dev-mode uvicorn watches the full `backend/` tree** — every save in `scripts/` or `tests/` triggers a reload that drops in-flight inference. Iterating on the bench script while it runs is incompatible with the dev backend.

**Path forward (next session, ~4 hours focused work):**
1. Write a **standalone Python bench** that imports `app.services.olmoearth_ft.load_ft_model` + `app.services.olmoearth_inference.run_ft_inference` directly. No HTTP, no uvicorn dependency, no reload-on-edit. Reuses the existing tested code paths.
2. **Pre-cache S2 imagery for the val subset before the bench runs** (the dataset tar has labels only — no Sentinel-2 layers under `windows/.../layers/`, so every inference call hits live STAC).
3. Run on **30 hash-stable val samples first** as smoke. Expect 30–90 min wall time including STAC fetch.
4. If clean, scale to 200–500 val samples (~3–8 h, run overnight) for stable per-class IoU.
5. AWF: same script, swap `--dataset awf` and the model repo. Larger dataset but same structure.
6. Report per-class IoU + class confusion + acceptance rate. **That's the real product number.**

**Why the standalone path is better:**
- Skips uvicorn HTTP layer (no reload sensitivity).
- Skips tile-render → PNG-decode → nearest-color-lookup detour (operates on raw `class_raster: np.ndarray` directly → exact mIoU, not RGB-quantised).
- Easier to add S2 fetch caching (rslearn windows in `mangrove.tar` could be cached locally to skip STAC for repeat runs).

### Two bench attempts during the autonomous overnight pass (real outcomes)

**Attempt 1 (HTTP, fresh model load).** Backend had been running through the entire TIPSv2 ADE20K bench session — B/14, L/14, g/14 + multi-scale all loaded across the night. When the OlmoEarth FT-Mangrove path tried to allocate GPU memory for its forward, it hit `CUDA error: out of memory` (`cudaErrorMemoryAllocation`), then `CUBLAS_STATUS_INTERNAL_ERROR` on subsequent attempts (the OOM left cuBLAS in a corrupted state). Real inference fell back to stub for every sample. **Lesson:** OlmoEarth FT runs need a clean uvicorn worker with empty GPU state — they can't share VRAM with an active TIPSv2 session on a 24 GB card.

**Attempt 2 (HTTP, after `preview_stop` + `preview_start` to clear GPU).** GPU was clean (0 / 24463 MiB) at backend restart. But the live S2 fetch path took 900+ seconds and timed out (`chunk 1/1 TIMED OUT after 900s (network stuck?)`). All chunks failed, real inference fell back to stub again. The stub deterministic-gradient output is decoded by the bench as garbage classes ("Other" → "water", "Water" → "nodata"). **Lesson:** with the dataset tar shipping labels-only, every inference call hits live STAC; without pre-cached S2 imagery for the val subset, network instability is a hard blocker.

**Net result for OlmoEarth labelling accuracy: 0 real-model predictions, no usable mIoU number.** The bench scaffold is shippable and correct (unit-tested helper code; legend-anchor decoder verified against the colormap math); the blocker is purely environmental. To actually produce a number a future session needs to:
1. Stop the running backend before benching (no shared-VRAM contention).
2. Either run when STAC (Element84 / Planetary Computer) is healthy, or pre-cache S2 imagery for ~30 val samples by running the existing `sentinel2_fetch.py` for each `(lon, lat, 2020-01-01/2020-12-31)` triple ahead of time. Cache lives in `backend/data/s2_cache/` and would survive bench restart.
3. Run the standalone Python bench (skip HTTP) so a single STAC retry doesn't take down the whole worker.

**Estimated time once the env is right: 2–4 hours for ~30-sample Mangrove + Mangrove pre-cache; another 1–2 days for AWF (76.9k samples → realistically 500 val sample subsample at ~5 h compute).**
